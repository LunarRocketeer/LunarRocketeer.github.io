Writing is a something that's been with me since forever. As early as kindergarden I was being noticed for it, but more importantly I had already started to enjoy it. Wether it be short stories, school essays, or screenplays, putting pen to paper and finger to keyboard is something that brings me great joy and pride. Sharing my work through spoken word and presentation is something I've started to become good at later in life, but already I think I do a good job at delivering my ideas to a crowd in a clear, interesting, and entertaining way.

Since writing comes up in everything I do, this page is kind of just a catch-all for the things that didn't fit elsewhere. My academic writings, for example, are on the Geography page. Also, there unfortunately exists few recordings of any of my presentations, so you'll jus thave to take my word for it that I'm really great.

After Everything

2020

A short story based on the following writing prompt from reddit: You are immortal. After your body can no longer be supported in any form on any planet due to the universe entering the final stages of heat death, you finally understand - you are to become the next "God", due to you existing before the next universe's big bang and being unable to die.

Still not sure how I feel about the ending, but I guess the demise of the universe is never going to live up to our expectations, is it?

I might still do that someday, who knows, but a lot of other things have made it further up the list since then. Even so, I'm putting this essay here largely for my own benefit. Graduation Speech

2016

My final two years of high school were when I started to actually enjoy public speaking and presenting. Despite the near panic-attack state I sometimes enter prior, sharing my thoughts with others, either to simply explain or to open myself up to improvement, is something I began having a lot of fun with! So when it came time to have auditions for speeches and perforamcnes at our graduation, I knew I had to try my harest to get the spotlight. After some feedback from my English teacher, I went up on the practice stage and delivered my speech, and after some debate from the judges (as I was told later) and some more speech-edits, I managed to get that spotlight. I think the piece lost a bit of focus at the end, it didn't quite hit the resolution that it should have, but the words I delivered that night continue to come to my mind as I keep growing up, and the real end to that speech has really only just begun to reveal itself to me.
